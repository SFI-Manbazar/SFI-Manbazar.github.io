20th SFI Manbazar Local Committee Conference Elects New Leadership Team

The 20th SFI Manbazar Local Committee Conference took place on January 4, 2024, at the CPI(M) Zonal Office in Manbazar, Podder Para. This conference marked a significant event for the local student community as they gathered to discuss critical issues and elect new leadership for the upcoming term.

The previous leadership team consisted of Comrade Pratik Chakroborty, who served as the Secretory, and Comrade Anik Narayan Deb, who was the President. Both were elected during the 19th SFI Manbazar Local Committee Conference. The 20th conference saw several new faces join the leadership team, ensuring a fresh and energetic approach to addressing the concerns of students in the region.

The conference was attended by prominent district leaders from SFI Purulia District Committee. Comrade Subrata Mahato, the District Committee Secretary, and Comrade Sayantan Ghosh, the District Committee President, played an active role in guiding the discussions. District Committee Member Comrade Saikat Sengupta also participated, offering valuable insights to the attending student comrades.

A major highlight of the conference was the election of the new local committee. After thorough discussions and consultations, a committee consisting of 25 dedicated student comrades was formed. Comrade Pabitra Banerjee was elected as the new Local Committee Secretary, taking over from Comrade Pratik Chakroborty. This marks a new chapter in the leadership of the SFI Manbazar Local Committee.

Comrade Anik Narayan Deb retained his position as the Local Committee President, providing continuity in leadership. The role of Joint Secretary was taken up by Comrade Bidyadhar Bauri, while Comrade Dibbyendu Chand was elected as the Vice President, both of whom bring enthusiasm and determination to the team. The position of cashier was filled by Comrade Saikat Mukherjee, who will handle the financial responsibilities of the committee.

During the conference, all attending comrades took an important oath — “study first, struggle must.” This commitment highlights their focus on academic excellence while remaining dedicated to fighting for the rights of students. The newly elected committee made it clear that their top priority would be to address the educational challenges faced by students in their area.

One of the key decisions made during the conference was to launch a local campaign advocating for students' educational rights. The new committee aims to fight against various obstacles that hinder access to quality education in the region. This includes addressing issues such as inadequate educational facilities, the need for better infrastructure, and equal access to resources for all students.

The conference also served as a platform for discussion on larger national and regional educational policies, with student leaders voicing their concerns about the state of education in the country. They emphasized the importance of unity and collective struggle in ensuring that every student, regardless of their background, has access to education that is both affordable and of high quality.
